Things to consider before choosing a laptop!!

  • Processor

    • A good processor is the thing you would want the most if you are looking for laptop capable of doing all your works seamlessly. Among many available processor in the market I suggest you to atleast go for higher gen i5 (i5 7th gen and above) or AMD ryzen 5 and above. The best option will, however, be the i5 and i7 10/11th gen or AMD Ryzen 5 and Ryzen 7 (4000/5000) series.

    To compare and know more about processors click here..

  • Storage and RAM

    • There are two types of storage available in the market SSD(Solid State Drive) and HDD (Hard disk drive) SSD is much faster compared to HDD of same storage capacity while its lot more expensive as well. Hence, I will recommend you to get a laptop with SSD 256-512 gb and 1TB Hdd and its availabilty varies with models as all laptops may not come with both SSD and HDDs in that case you can always go for additional external HDD.

    In terms of RAM 8 GB should be the least you should be looking at, while its enough for most of the tasks you will do, spending a couple thousands more for 16 GB of RAM would be better in long term

  • Battery Life

    • For any student a good battery life is a very important to complete your work both in and out of your home. Usually ultrabooks have better battery life compared to gaming laptops. Hence, I suggest you to not buy a gaming laptop unless you are going to spend most of your time playing high end games or creating 4K contents every other day.

  • Display and Ports


    • While display might not be most important thing for a student, its still better to know about it before buying because in the long use poor display quality and low brightness may give you some headache. Hence, laptops with FHD 60Hz display with IPS panel and brightness of around 300 nits is the minimum you should be looking for. Also keep in mind Matte or anti glare displays are better for using it outdoors but if you know you are gonna use it indoors the most, then you can always go for glossy panels.

    About ports, most people give a blind eye to it but in the long term you will realise how important a dedicated port is as you wont have to carry around those annoying adaptors and card readers. Make sure your laptop atleast has ports for USB C, USB A, HDMI and headphone jack.
